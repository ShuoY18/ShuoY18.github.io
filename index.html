Abstract

Video generation using diffusion-based models is typically constrained by high computational costs due to the complex interactions inherent in video data. This paper addresses these efficiency challenges by optimizing the denoising process across frames. Our key discovery is that inter-frame motion features remain consistent throughout the majority of the diffusion process and can be characterized as coarse-grained. Leveraging this insight, we propose a lightweight motion learning module that efficiently captures and utilizes these inter-frame motions to transform and generate subsequent frames. This module enables the reuse of frames across multiple denoising steps, significantly reducing the need to regenerate each frame from scratch, thus decreasing the computational load and accelerating the video generation process. We apply frame-specific updates only in the final stages of denoising to preserve the integrity and detail of the video. A comprehensive evaluation across various tasks, including style transfer, video prediction, and interpolation, demonstrates that our method substantially reduces computational demands while simultaneously enhancing the speed and quality of video content generation.
